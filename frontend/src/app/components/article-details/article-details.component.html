Sleep is essential for our physical and mental well-being. In Ayurveda, sleep (Nidra) is considered one of the three pillars of life, alongside food (Ahara) and managed celibacy (Brahmacharya).

Understanding Sleep Cycles

Modern science tells us that while we sleep, our brains remain active, cycling through different stages including REM and deep sleep.

"Early to bed and early to rise makes a man healthy, wealthy, and wise." This aligns perfectly with Ayurvedic wisdom.

Practical Tips

Stick to a Schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day to regulate your internal clock.

Bedtime Ritual

Warm milk with nutmeg, foot massage, or gentle breathing exercises.

Optimize Environment

Keep bedroom dark, quiet, and cool. Use calming oils like lavender.
