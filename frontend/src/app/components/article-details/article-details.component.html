Sleep is essential for our physical and mental well-being. In Ayurveda, sleep (Nidra) is considered one of the three pillars of life, alongside food (Ahara) and managed celibacy (Brahmacharya). Quality sleep regenerates our tissues and restores balance to our doshas.

Understanding Sleep Cycles

Modern science tells us that while we sleep, our brains remain active, cycling through different stages including REM (rapid eye movement) and deep sleep. Ayurveda adds that the time we go to bed significantly impacts the quality of this restoration.

"Early to bed and early to rise makes a man healthy, wealthy, and wise." - This old adage aligns perfectly with Ayurvedic wisdom.

Practical Tips for Better Rest

  • Stick to a schedule: Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day to regulate your internal clock.
  • Create a bedtime ritual: Warm milk with nutmeg, a foot massage, or gentle breathing exercises can signal your body it's time to rest.
  • Optimize your environment: Keep your bedroom dark, quiet, and cool. Consider using calming essential oils like lavender.
